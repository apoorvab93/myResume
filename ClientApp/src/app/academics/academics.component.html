Projects

An Optimal Control Strategy for Earth-Moon Low-Thrust Spacecraft Trajectory Design, Electrical Engineering Department, Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN

  • An open loop Guidance used pre-optimized control history to propagate both attitude dynamics and translational dynamics with given Guidance command and Controller.
  • There were 3 major parts to the project:
    • Optimization: Minimize the propellant consumption of CubeSat, i.e. maximizing the mass.
    • Guidance: Read table of optimized unit thrust vector and generate quaternion error to be passed in controller for proper vehicle attitude.
    • Control: Take quaternion and angular velocity errors and drive them to zero ensuring system stability.

LiDAR Video Integration, Graduate Project, The Autonomous Systems Lab, Purdue University
  • Developed an algorithm that superimposes the LiDAR video onto the camera video for traffic monitoring.

System Identification of a Quadcopter, Graduate Project, The Autonomous Systems Lab, Purdue University

Coordinated Control and Command of Unmanned Underwater Vehicles, Senior Design Project, University of Connecticut, Storrs, CT

  • Built a fleet of Unmanned Underwater Vehicles that can communicate with each other and localize in real time.
  • Developed leader-follower algorithm and validated in Player/Stage before pool and lake testing.
  • Existing GUI was enhanced to send commands to the UUVs to perform movement and localization processes.

Sound Transmission using a Laser Pointer, University of Connecticut, Storrs, CT

  • Designed a circuit to transmit sound using a laser pointer to a minimum distance of 30 feet where the transmitter circuit was built using a voltage regulator and the receiver circuit was a PIN diode.
